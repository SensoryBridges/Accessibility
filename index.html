Accessibility Statement

Sensory Bridges, Inc.
Last Updated: March 22, 2026

Our Commitment

Sensory Bridges, Inc. is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. As a company that builds assistive technology for neurodivergent individuals, accessibility is central to our mission. We continually work to improve the user experience for everyone and apply the relevant accessibility standards across our website and products.

Standards

We strive to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2, Level AA, published by the Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI) of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). As of the date of this statement, the Site has not undergone a formal third-party accessibility audit. Sensory Bridges is committed to completing a formal WCAG 2.2 Level AA conformance assessment and publishing the results on this page. In the interim, we actively test the Site for accessibility using both automated tools and manual review.

We also strive to comply with Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 (29 U.S.C. § 794d) and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) as applicable to our digital content.

Measures Taken

We take the following measures to ensure accessibility of the Site:

  • Semantic HTML structure with appropriate heading hierarchy.
  • Alternative text for meaningful images.
  • Sufficient color contrast ratios for text and interactive elements.
  • Keyboard navigability for all interactive elements.
  • Screen reader compatibility.
  • Responsive design that functions across devices and screen sizes.
  • Accessible forms with properly associated labels.
  • Clear and consistent navigation.

Compatibility

The Site is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen readers (including NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, and TalkBack).
  • Screen magnification software.
  • Speech recognition software.
  • Keyboard-only navigation.

The Site is designed to function with current versions of the following browsers: Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Apple Safari, and Microsoft Edge.

Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some content on the Site may not yet be fully accessible. We are actively working to identify and resolve any accessibility barriers. Known limitations include:

  • Some embedded third-party content (including scheduling widgets and form embeds) may have accessibility limitations outside of our direct control. We work with third-party providers that prioritize accessibility and will seek alternatives where current providers fall short.
  • Some PDF documents may not be fully accessible to screen readers. We are working to remediate existing documents and ensure all new documents meet accessibility standards.
